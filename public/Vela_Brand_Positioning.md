# Vela — Brand Positioning

---

## Ideal Audience

**All long-haul cabin crew, 21–50 years old.** Every rank, every airline, every level of experience. iOS first, UAE-based launch, expanding globally. Direct-to-crew, paid app with a free trial.

The biology is the same whether it's your first year or your twentieth. The problem doesn't get easier with experience — it just becomes more familiar.

---

## Pain Points

### Involuntary Microsleep During Critical Phases
The most dangerous and most unspoken problem in the industry. During taxi, takeoff, and landing — the exact moments crew must be alert — they can be running on 24+ hours of wakefulness, deep in a circadian trough. The eyes close. Not from lack of professionalism. Because human biology at that point cannot be overridden by willpower. Getting caught means immediate termination. The fear is constant. The fatigue wins anyway.

### Red-Eye Flights With Nothing to Fight It
The cabin is dark. Passengers are asleep. There's nothing to do. The body interprets every cue as a signal to sleep — and it's right, except being asleep is career-ending. Crew sit there fighting their own physiology for hours with no tools, no warning, and no support.

### Irregular Wake Times Hitting Circadian Troughs
It's not just tiredness from a long shift. It's being woken at 02:00 body time — the lowest point of the human alertness cycle — and then performing safety-critical duties. This is a fundamentally different kind of fatigue that sleep alone the night before cannot fix.

### The Body Clock Problem
Crew don't just cross time zones — they cross them repeatedly, in different directions, without consistent recovery time. Nobody tells them when to sleep before a Sydney turn or how badly a Johannesburg layover will hit them. They figure it out through trial, error, and exhaustion.

### Rosters Are Unreadable as Wellbeing Data
A roster tells you where to be and when. It tells you nothing about what that pattern is doing to your body. Crew have to mentally calculate "I land at 06:00 DXB but my body thinks it's 02:00 and I start again in 31 hours" — and most don't, because it's hard.

### The Tools That Exist Aren't Built for Them
Generic sleep apps assume a 9-to-5 life. Fitness trackers measure but don't interpret. Nothing connects the roster to the body. Crew end up on WhatsApp groups, Facebook forums, asking AI, searching online — cobbling together answers that were never designed for their situation.

### Fatigue Affects Performance and Safety — and Crew Know It
Crew are acutely aware that tiredness affects their judgment, their service, and in emergencies, their safety decisions. This is a professional anxiety, not just a personal comfort issue.

### The Job Identity Tension
Cabin crew take pride in always being "on." Admitting fatigue or struggling with wellbeing can feel like weakness in a profession that selects for resilience. They need a tool that feels like a performance edge, not a health complaint.

## Purpose of Existence

Most crew have no idea where their body clock actually is. They know they're tired. They don't know why at a physiological level, what's coming next, or what they can actually do about it. That gap — between experiencing fatigue and understanding it — is where Vela lives.

Vela was built by crew, for crew. Founded by a cabin crew member who lived this problem firsthand, partnered with a software developer, and powered by a circadian engine backed by science from NASA, the US Navy, and real personal experiences from crew members around the world. This isn't adapted from a corporate wellness tool. It was built from the inside out.

**Vela exists to give crew two things they've never had: understanding and control.**

**Understanding** — so crew can see exactly where their body clock is at any point in their roster. Why that Johannesburg layover always destroys them. When their alertness will peak and when it will collapse. The science that was previously only available to airline operations planners, now in the palm of your hand in plain language.

**Control** — because understanding without action is just anxiety. Vela doesn't just explain, it tells you what to do. Sleep now. Avoid bright light tonight. This is your danger window — here is how to prepare for it. Specific, practical, personal.

The roster has always run crew. It dictates everything and accounts for nothing — not their health, not their recovery, not their life outside the aircraft. Crew have had no choice but to comply. Vela doesn't change the roster. It changes the crew member's relationship with it. They stop being passive victims of the schedule and start making informed decisions within it.

The goal isn't just functionality. It's crew who are energised on layovers, present on days off, and genuinely free outside of work. Not just surviving the schedule — living alongside it.

**That is the purpose. Empowerment through knowledge. Taking your life back from a system that was never designed to protect you.**
